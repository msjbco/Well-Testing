Helpful information about well testing, regulations, and water quality in Colorado

Understanding Well Testing Requirements

Most lenders in Colorado require well testing before approving a home loan. The specific requirements vary by loan type and county, but typically include:

  • Flow Rate Test: Proves your well can produce enough water (usually 3-5 GPM, minimum, sustained)
  • Bacteria Test: Ensures water is free from coliform and E.coli bacteria
  • Water Quality Panel: Tests for contaminants like arsenic, lead, uranium, and nitrates
  • Loan-Specific Tests: Some loans require radon in water or additional testing

Colorado Well Water Regulations

Colorado has specific regulations for well water quality. Key limits include:

  • Coliform & E.coli: Must be absent (0 colonies per 100mL)
  • Nitrate: EPA limit is 10 mg/L (higher levels can cause health issues)
  • Arsenic: EPA limit is 10 ppb (parts per billion)
  • Lead: EPA action level is 15 ppb
  • Uranium: EPA limit is 30 ppb
  • Radon in Water: Colorado action level is 4,000 pCi/L

Common Questions

How long does well testing take?

Flow rate tests typically take 3 hours on-site. Lab results for bacteria and water quality are usually available within 24-48 hours.

What if my well fails a test?

We can help! For bacteria issues, we offer on-site shock chlorination. For other contaminants, we'll provide recommendations for treatment systems or remediation options.

Do I need testing if I'm not buying/selling?

Regular well testing (annually or every 2-3 years) is recommended to ensure your family's water is safe. Many contaminants have no taste or smell.
